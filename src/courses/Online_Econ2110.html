Course Description

Welcome! In this online course, we will use real-world data to teach introductory macroeconomics. We’ll begin by exploring important questions in macroeconomics and familiarizing ourselves with the current state of the US economy. Then, we’ll learn how to analyze a macroeconomic issue using quantitative tools and economic theory. An economy is far too complex to understand without a general framework. We will use a simple framework based on the law of demand and supply. This course aims to develop your ability to think, write, and speak like a macroeconomist.

***This course is completely online and delivered through Canvas. Please log in to your Canvas course page and become familiar with the platform. Access to a computer and a reliable internet connection is required to complete the course. Each 3-credit course at UNM is designed to engage students for a minimum of 135 hours throughout the semester. For this online intersession class, which lasts approximately three weeks, you should be prepared to dedicate about 45 hours of your time each week to this course. If you are not ready to commit the required time now, you should reconsider your decision. I also offer a 16-week version of this class during the Fall and Spring semesters.***

Textbook

Mankiw, N. Gregory. 2021. Principles of Macroeconomics. 9th ed. Mason, OH: CENGAGE Learning Custom Publishing.

Student Learning Goals

After successful completion of this course, students will achieve the following:

  • Familiarity with basic facts about the U.S. economy: FRED BEA Opportunity Insights
  • Sound understanding of important macroeconomic questions: WSJ NYT The Economist
  • Proficiency in using quantitative tools and economic theory
  • Knowledge in growth models, aggregate consumption/savings, macro-finance, monetary policy, fiscal policy, and international macroeconomics
  • The ability to explain any macroeconomic issue intuitively, mathematically, and graphically

Assessment

Exams

There will be two exams, each contributing 30% to the total grade. Exam 1 will cover Module 1 (Problem Sets 1-5 + Relevant Textbook chapters), while Exam 2 will focus on Module 2 (Problem Sets 6-10 + Relevant Textbook chapters). Each exam will consist of 30 multiplechoice questions, which you must complete within 60 minutes. You will take the exam via Canvas on the scheduled date (check the schedule section for exam dates). The exams will be available in Canvas for 24 hours. You may complete them at your convenience during this time; however, once you begin, you must finish within 60 minutes.

Problem Sets

Problem sets constitute 30% of the total grade. This course will have ten problem sets, each carrying a 3% weight. All problem sets (and their answer keys) are posted on my website. You must handwrite your work in a physical and/or digital notebook. Next, submit your work in PDF format. You can take pictures or scan your work and create a PDF file using free scanning apps on your phone, then upload it via Canvas. Do not type your assignment using a computer or electronic device, such as MS Word or similar software. Please check the Schedule section in this syllabus for submission deadlines.

Reading Assignments

Readings are worth 10% of the total grade. A total of five reading assignments will be asked in this semester. All reading assignments will be posted on my website. You can handwrite/typewrite your work in a physical and/or digital notebook. Next, submit your work in PDF format. You can take pictures or scan your work and create a PDF file using free scanning apps on your phone, then upload it via Canvas.

Late Submissions/Make-up Policy

There are penalties for late completion of your assignments/exams via Canvas. The Canvas submission link may expire if you cannot meet the deadline. All Canvas submissions are due by 11:59 pm. Check the schedule section for all deadlines. A 20 percent penalty will be applied for each day that the submission/exam is late. If you complete your assignment/exam a few minutes late, such as at 12:02 AM or 12:03 AM, it will be considered late by one day, resulting in a 20 percent deduction from your total score.

Extenuating Circumstance Policy: In the unusual instance that you need further accommodations than what is listed above, you must meet the following conditions:

You must send your request to me within 24 hours of the assignment’s original due date with the requested documentation:

  • Medical Emergency: If you require an extension because of a medical emergency, you need to provide a letter from a doctor with dates of excused absences and expected return date.
  • Military Obligations: If you require further accommodation because of an unexpected military obligation, you need to provide a military order of the dates of excused absences.
  • Death of an immediate family member: If you request an extension because of the death of an immediate family member, there is no need to provide me with documentation. Your extension is increased to 5 days in accordance with UNM bereavement policy
